Top 5 Streamers from Fortnite

On this page I will comment 5 of the biggest Fortnite streamers in the world.

Clix


Clix was born in the United States, he plays Fortnite professionally, for NRG Esports, he played in the Fortnite World Cup and there he won over $ 160,000, he has won over $ 90,000 in online championships, and he is still the 3rd best player North America - East server. On his YouTube channel he has more than 1.3 million subscribers and on Twitch more than 2.5 million followers, and his streams have been watched for more than 3.5 million hours, counting only the month of October 2020.

Benjyfishy


Known as Benjyfishy, ​​he plays professionally for NRG, was born in England and holds the 1st position as the best server player in Europe. Benjyfishy played in the Fortnite World Cup, where he ranked 25th in solo mode and 14th in duos, and has won nearly $ 400,000 in online tournaments. He got more than 1.3 million subscribers on YouTube and more than 2.3 million followers on Twitch, in addition to getting almost 2.5 million hours watched on his streams in October 2020.

Mongraal


Mongraal is a player from Europe, was born in England, played professionally for Team Secret, but in 2019 he joined another organization, FaZe Clan. He played in the Fortnite World Cup, and got top 6 in duos and top 13 in solo mode, he was not known just because of his skill, others factors was his tilts and rages in streams, which in a way were very shared. He has more than 3.6 million subscribers on YouTube and more than 3.4 million followers on Twitch, has more than 2.3 million hours watched in his streams in October 2020 alone.

Bugha


Bugha was born in the United States, currently plays for the Sentinels team, he was well known mainly for his 1st place victory in the World Cup in solo mode, thus earning US $ 3,000,000, after winning the Fortnite World Cup he it quickly gained many followers on all its social networks, currently having almost 3.5 million subscribers on YouTube, almost 3.4 million followers on Twitch and had almost 1.5 million hours watched in his streams during October 2020.

Fresh


Australian MrFreshAsian, better known as "Fresh", came from the game Team Fortress 2, where he trained hard and became a great player, left TF2 and started broadcasting and playing Fortnite, and due to his skills, it didn't take long for to be noticed and grow on Twitch. He was part of Team Renegade, but now he is part of Luminostity Gaming and has already won more than US $ 30,000 in open championships. In October 2020, he had almost 1.5 million hours watched in his streams, in addition to having more than 6.5 million subscribers on YouTube and almost 3.5 million followers on Twitch.
